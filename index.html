Products (Consumer Goods) & Supply Chain

Logistics Network Optimization

Supply Chain Analyst | Network Simulation Case

The Challenge: A distributor of bulky consumer electronics (Washing Machines) faced high variable costs due to inefficient routing. The objective was to model a more efficient network distribution from the Central DC (Kudus).

  • Algorithmic Route Planning: Applied Saving Matrix and Nearest Insert algorithms to transition from a manual "Convoy" model to a "Cluster-based" system, maximizing vehicle space utilization.
  • Scenario Modeling: Developed a dynamic cost calculator integrating variable assumptions (Fuel, Labor, Tolls) to simulate financial outcomes under different routing scenarios.
  • Sustainability & Cost Impact: The simulation projected a 26.93% potential efficiency gain (Cost & Fuel), demonstrating how optimizing logistics networks theoretically correlates to reduced Carbon Footprint.
